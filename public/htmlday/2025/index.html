SEAL FACTS

  • their scientific name is 'phocids' and they are a type of pinniped. other pinnipeds are sea lions and walruses
  • they are mammals and their young are called pups
  • they evolved from mustelids (weasels, otters, raccoons, etc.) and their closest living relatives are bears
  • there are about 19 species of seals such as harbour seals, harp seals, and weddel seals
  • weddel seals sound like this
  • they do a 'banana' pose to minimise contact between their body and surface. this exposes more of their body to the air, allowing them to regulate their body temperature
  • they move around on land using a bouncing motion called galumphing
  • their bodies are streamlined to help them swim faster
  • their milk is very rich in fat and calories so that pups can grow nice and plump to help them galumph and stay warm
  • monk seals got their name from their belly folds which supposedly look like a monk's robe
  • they are native to colder areas like the arctic and antarctic waters. no seals near southeast asia =(
